Hi! Welcome to my page!


I currently work as a postdoctoral researcher within the project PASDCODE (Prosody AS a Dynamic COordinative DEvice). My research interests center around the perception-production relationship and the effect of language-specific prosody on sensorimotor control in speech production.

I finished my PhD at Laboratoire de Phonétique et Phonologie (LPP, CNRS/Sorbonne Nouvelle, Paris, France) under the supervision of Cécile Fougeron and Leonardo Lancia in 2023. My PhD dissertation focuses on how speech rhythm adapts to different speech production situations, i.e., those where speakers' auditory feedback is altered. I have also explored how the speech production situation could affect speakers' sense of agency relative to their voice.


I have a bachelor degree in French Linguistics, Literature, and Civilization, a master degree in Language Acquisition and Pathology, and another master degree in Français Langue Étrangère (FLE, French for French as a foreign language).
